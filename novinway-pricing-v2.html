Perspective

What does it cost to not have this?

More than half of parents raising neurodivergent children reduce their work hours or leave the workforce entirely - and the financial impact falls almost entirely on mothers. The cost compounds: $500,000 to $1,000,000+ in lifetime earnings, retirement, and career momentum. For the mothers still working, those raising children with autism earn 35-56% less than other mothers, solely because the logistics leave no room for anything else.

Novinway's childcare and coordination together cost a fraction of one lost or reduced month of income. The question isn't whether you can afford support. It's whether you can afford not to have it.

Common questions

What is a Care Profile?
Before your first visit, our behavior analyst gathers information about your child's routines, preferences, communication style, sensory needs, and what's worked (and hasn't) with previous caregivers. This profile becomes the training foundation for your caregiver. It's updated as your child grows and needs change.
Do I need both services?
No. Each service works independently. Some families only need childcare. Some only need coordination. Some use both. If you do use both, childcare members receive Family Support at a reduced rate - but there's no requirement to bundle.
What happens if my caregiver is unavailable?
Membership families receive a replacement guarantee. If your primary caregiver can't make a scheduled shift, we provide a trained backup who has access to your child's Care Profile. Flex families receive best-effort backup support.
Why don't you accept insurance?
Insurance covers clinical services like ABA therapy. Novinway is entirely nonclinical - we provide childcare and family coordination, not therapy. Operating outside the insurance model lets us stay flexible and focused on your family's actual needs rather than what a plan will authorize.
Can I use my FSA or dependent care benefits?
Many families are able to use dependent care flexible spending accounts (FSAs) or other pretax benefits to help cover the cost of childcare services. Check with your plan administrator for eligibility details.
What does the Family Support Coordinator actually do?
Whatever neurodivergent-specific logistics you're currently handling yourself. That might include provider scheduling, waiver applications, waitlist follow-up, program research, transportation logistics, or coordinating your childcare schedule around therapy and school. The scope depends on your family's situation - if a neurotypical family wouldn't have the problem, it's in scope.
What if I need an advocate or attorney?
Your Family Support Coordinator maintains relationships with vetted IEP advocates, special education attorneys, and other specialists in the DC metro area. When a situation comes up that needs that kind of support - an IEP meeting, a school placement dispute, a legal question - they connect you to the right person. You don't have to find them yourself.
Can you help me navigate clinical options?
Novinway is entirely nonclinical - we don't provide therapy, and we don't direct clinical decisions. But your Family Support Coordinator maintains relationships with vetted specialists across the DC metro area and can connect you to the right person when you need one.
Is there paperwork to get started?
Yes. Before services begin, families sign a service agreement covering expectations, scheduling policies, and liability. We walk you through everything during your first conversation - no surprises.
Are there contracts or cancellation fees?
No long-term contracts for any service. Childcare memberships and Family Support are both month-to-month - cancel either with 30 days' notice. Individual membership bookings can be cancelled with 24-hour notice at no penalty, and we work with families on emergencies and illness. Flex bookings require 48-hour notice.
